PROJECT OVERVIEW

visuEats is a restaurant menu app that focuses on stimulating the users appetites through highly visual menus, with features that provide an interactive and personalized experience.

SCOPE

I designed the first iteration of the mobile app and admin panel in 2018 alongside my friend and Founder of the company Sophronia Mckenzie, who also doubles as a “passionate foodie”. Not being able to see images of meals on the traditional restaurant menu was the major pain point in her experience that birthed the concept of the visuEats app. Originally, the idea was to provide restaurants with a tablet that was preloaded with the software would be created and provided by visuEats, but after thoughtful consideration it was decided that a mobile app was a more feasible direction.

THE PROBLEM

Restaurant diners have a hard time visualizing meals listed on traditional paper menus, this makes the decision process longer and creates a high chance of disappointment, as they are relying solely on imagination to visualize the meals.

DESIGN OVERVIEW
THE KICK OFF

In order to gain empathy within a short time frame, I decided to use Sophronia (Founder of visuEats) as the persona in the beginning phase of this project. Her passion for food and love of a great dining out experience, made her qualified for the role as the “ideal user”. The train of thought here, was that if the app met her needs, it was good enough to launch.

MEET SOPHRONIA

CREATING THE SOLUTION
ANALYSING THE COMPETITORS

Mayber’s decision to provide the users with a search filter to find restaurants nearby was great for users who had no clue what to eat. The use of tiles as a design element as well as the decision to prioritize the images of meals gave users a clear view of what to expect in their order. This solution eases the frustration of people who currently order from traditional menus. Yelp provided a visual guideline for us, as their use of cards to display meals and meal description is somewhat a standard in the industry so taking this approach would ensure that we created a design that was familiar to our users.

“The biggest room in the world, is the room for improvement”

A list of requirements that was provided was used to design the first mid-fidelity prototype that was created for a tablet. However, after quick consideration it was decided that providing both hardware and software was not feasible in the short timeframe.

Once it was identified that it was not going to be feasible to provide the hardware, the focus was quickly shifted to the creation of a mobile app. The app would allow users to access a restaurant’s menu from their mobile phones by downloading the app or scanning a QR code to access the menu. The first prototype allows users to view high-resolution images of the menu items as well as view the description and prices of items just like you would, using a traditional menu. Users can also select their language preference, filter the menu based on any food restrictions or diet, as well as leave and view restaurant ratings.

DESIGN DECISIONS

As it relates to the design, the objective was to design the app so that it was easy to use and as well as easy to understand.

Color Scheme

I maintained the visuEats branding throughout the onboarding process and general search screen so that users can differentiate between general search & the restaurant’s menu..

Tiles

The use of tiles were implemented as a way to provide mobile users with painless touch points..

Text / Typography

Choosing to use the visuEats brand typography style throughout the design was an early rookie mistake that had to be quickly rectified during the development with use of a web font. .

ON THE HORIZON

The design process for version 2.0 of the visuEats web-based menu and mobile app is currently underway. Methodologies such as 1:1 user interviews, surveys and user testing will be used to gain a deeper understanding of the preferences as well as frustrations of our target users and hopefully uncover new insights that can be used to inform our future design decisions, prioritize features and potentially increase the value of visuEats.
